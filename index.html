About the Telescope

The telescope is an optical instrument which magnifies distant objects, opening new horizons for science and exploration.

Before its invention, our understanding of the universe was limited to naked-eye observations.

History of the Invention

  • Hans Lippershey (1608): Credited with the first telescope design.
  • Galileo Galilei: Enhanced the design and made astronomical discoveries.
  • Johannes Kepler: Improved telescope optics and theory.

These figures shaped the course of observational astronomy forever.

How It Works

Telescopes gather and magnify light using lenses or mirrors to focus it into an image.

Images can illustrate the mechanics behind these powerful tools of discovery.

Impact on Science

From Galileo's moons of Jupiter to Hubble's deep space images, telescopes have profoundly altered our understanding of the universe.

They have unveiled wonders of space, proved theories, and inspired generations of scientists and explorers.

Modern Telescopes

Today's telescopes are capable of exploring the farthest reaches of space.

Examples include:

  • Hubble Space Telescope: Provided groundbreaking images of the cosmos.
  • James Webb Space Telescope: Exploring infrared light for new discoveries.
